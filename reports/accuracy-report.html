How Accurate Is the AI?

We created 6 types of simulated patients — each with a different emotional trajectory (improving, worsening, volatile, etc.). The AI read their journal entries and tried to detect their mood and anxiety levels without knowing the answers in advance.

Mood: Strong Performance

Average error of 0.57 points on a 1–10 scale. The AI reliably distinguishes "good days" from "bad days."

Mood Accuracy 94%

Anxiety: Room to Improve

Average error of 1.36 points. Anxiety is less explicitly expressed in free-text journaling than mood.

Anxiety Accuracy 85%
Why is anxiety harder? When people journal, they tend to describe how they feel (sad, happy, hopeful) more directly than their anxiety level. Anxiety often manifests through indirect signals like sleep disruption, racing thoughts, or avoidance — which are harder for AI to quantify as a single score. This is consistent with clinical literature and represents an active area of improvement.

Can It Learn Individual Patterns?

The system builds a personalized model for each user, learning the relationship between their journal themes, sleep, energy, and self-reported mood. We tested this across 45 users by training on 80% of their data and testing on the remaining 20%.

Personalized Prediction 91% accurate
What does this mean? After seeing ~30 journal entries from a person, the model can predict their self-reported mood within about 0.8 points. It also provides confidence intervals — and those intervals contained the true mood 69% of the time (target: 80%).

Methodology

Synthetic Ground Truth

Test data is generated from 6 clinically-motivated patient archetypes, each with a deterministic mood/anxiety trajectory over 30–60 days. Gaussian noise (std 0.6–1.2) is added for realism.

ArchetypeMood TrajectoryAnxiety TrajectoryClinical Analog

Volatile StabilizerOscillating, dampeningOscillating, dampeningEmotional dysregulation improving
Hidden Deteriorator6.5 → 3 (slow decline)3 → 7 (slow rise)Worsening masked by presentation
Flat Non-ResponderConstant ~4Constant ~6Treatment-resistant depression

Extraction Pipeline

Each journal entry is sent to GPT-4 Turbo (temperature 0.3, max 1000 tokens) with a structured system prompt requesting mood (1–10), anxiety (1–10), PHQ-9/GAD-7-aligned indicator checklists (0–3 per item), emotions, symptoms, triggers, a crisis flag, and character-level evidence spans. Response is parsed as JSON. Evidence spans are validated against the source text using exact substring matching with auto-repair of incorrect offsets.

Calibration Model

Per-user ridge regression (λ=1.0) maps AI-extracted features (affect valence/arousal, sleep hours/quality, energy, medication, and binary feature indicators for recurring themes) to self-reported mood labels. Bootstrap sampling (B=50) estimates per-prediction uncertainty. Evaluation uses an 80/20 chronological split. Metrics: MAE, 80% prediction interval coverage (normal approximation, z=1.28), and Expected Calibration Error (ECE, 10 bins over [1,10]).
